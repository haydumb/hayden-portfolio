## The problem

Anomalies in New Relic only mattered if someone happened to be looking at the right dashboard
at the right time. Important signals sat unseen until they became incidents, and the team had
no lightweight way to be told "something looks off" the moment it happened.

## The approach

I built an **anomaly-detection workflow with Power Automate** that watched New Relic for
anomalous behavior and, on detection, **pushed a real-time alert into Microsoft Teams via an
incoming webhook**. It turned a passive dashboard into an active signal — the data came to the
team instead of the team going to the data.

Keeping it in Power Automate made it transparent and maintainable: the whole flow is readable
and editable without a deploy, which suited a glue-layer tool that needs to evolve quickly.

## The impact

- **Real-time alerts** in the channel people already watch.
- **Faster awareness** of issues, with no dashboard babysitting.
- A clean, **no-code bridge** between observability and team communication.
