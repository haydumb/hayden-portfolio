## The problem

Anomalies in New Relic only helped if someone was looking at the right dashboard at the right
moment. Important signals sat there unnoticed until they turned into incidents, and the team had
no easy way to hear "something looks off" when it happened.

## The approach

I built a Power Automate workflow that watched New Relic for anomalies and posted an alert into
Microsoft Teams through an incoming webhook as soon as it caught one. Instead of people checking
the dashboard, the dashboard came to them.

Keeping it in Power Automate kept it easy to read and change. The whole flow is editable without a
deploy, which is what you want from glue like this.

## The impact

- Real-time alerts in the channel the team already watches.
- Faster awareness, with no dashboards to babysit.
- A simple, no-code bridge between observability and chat.
